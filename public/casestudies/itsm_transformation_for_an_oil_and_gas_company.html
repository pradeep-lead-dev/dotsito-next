Client Overview


Our client, a leading player in the global oil and gas industry, faced significant challenges with its IT service management (ITSM) processes. The company’s legacy ITSM systems were outdated, leading to inefficiencies, high operational costs, and poor service quality. The management decided to undertake a comprehensive ITSM transformation to address these issues.

The Challenge


The client’s existing ITSM systems were struggling to meet the demands of a global organization in a competitive and fast-paced industry. Key challenges included:

  • Inefficiencies in ITSM Processes: Legacy systems created bottlenecks and delays in IT service delivery.
  • High Operational Costs: The outdated systems resulted in high costs due to manual processes and poor automation.
  • Poor Service Quality: Service quality was inconsistent, and the IT support team struggled to meet performance expectations.

The Solution


We implemented a strategic ITSM transformation to address these challenges:


Assessment of Current ITSM Processes

We began by conducting a thorough assessment of the existing ITSM processes and systems across all business units. This allowed us to uncover inefficiencies, gaps, and areas for improvement.


Defining the Target ITSM Architecture

After the assessment, we worked with the client to define the target ITSM architecture. This architecture incorporated standardized global processes while considering necessary best practices. The design process included:

  • Optimized workflows tailored to the client’s needs.
  • Identifying required integrations with other systems.
  • Establishing both standard and custom process elements that would align with business objectives.

Refining ITSM Processes

We refined and developed ITSM processes to align with the newly defined target architecture. This phase ensured that processes were efficient, automated where possible, and met global standards for consistency.


Integrating Multilingual Support

Given the global nature of the client’s operations, we integrated multilingual support within the ServiceNow platform. This allowed for seamless IT service delivery across diverse linguistic regions and improved the user experience for non-English speaking employees.


Workflow Automation Using ServiceNow

We incorporated the updated workflows into the existing IT infrastructure and leveraged ServiceNow’s automation capabilities. This reduced manual tasks, streamlined workflows, and significantly enhanced operational efficiency.


Task Automation with Virtual Agent

We identified repetitive and manual tasks that could be automated using ServiceNow’s virtual agent. This virtual agent helped resolve common service requests, freeing up resources for more complex issues and improving service quality.

Results


The transformation led to measurable improvements:

  • Increased Operational Efficiency: Automation and optimized workflows reduced manual work, leading to faster and more efficient service delivery.
  • Cost Reduction: By replacing outdated systems with ServiceNow’s integrated and automated platform, the client was able to reduce operational costs.
  • Improved Service Quality: The introduction of standardized processes and multilingual support enhanced service consistency and quality across the organization.
  • Faster Issue Resolution: The virtual agent automated routine service requests, leading to quicker resolution times and better resource utilization.

Conclusion


The ITSM transformation project helped the client modernize its IT service management processes, align them with best practices, and create a more efficient, cost-effective IT environment. By leveraging ServiceNow’s automation and multilingual capabilities, the client not only improved operational efficiency but also provided a better experience for their global workforce. The results of the transformation were evident in the improved service delivery and reduced costs, setting the client on the path to continued success in a competitive industry.
