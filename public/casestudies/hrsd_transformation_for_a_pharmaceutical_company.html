Client Overview


Our client is a leading global pharmaceutical company with a workforce exceeding 100,000 employees, spread across various regions worldwide. Given this intricate landscape, the client sought to stabilize and optimize their HR management processes to enhance overall efficiency and employee experience. Their goals were twofold:

  • Process Optimization: Streamlining fragmented HR processes across decentralized business units by integrating systems, automating tasks, and standardizing procedures.

  • Improved Employee Experience: Enhancing HR interactions with intuitive self-service options and better communication channels to boost employee satisfaction and retention.

The Challenge


The client faced several challenges due to its global and decentralized structure, including:

  • Fragmented HR Processes: The HR processes across various business units were inconsistent and lacked standardization, leading to inefficiencies and confusion.

  • Manual Tasks: A significant amount of HR work was manual, leading to delays and errors in HR operations.

  • Regional Discrepancies: While global standardization was essential, the client also needed to ensure that local regulations and practices were accommodated in each region.

  • Employee Experience: The client aimed to improve employee engagement and retention, requiring better self-service HR options and streamlined communication.

The Solution


To address the challenges, we implemented a two-phase approach to optimize HR processes and improve the employee experience.


Phase 1: Assessment and Target Architecture Definition

In this phase, we conducted a thorough analysis of the client’s existing HR processes across all business units to identify inefficiencies and inconsistencies. Based on the findings, we defined the target HR architecture, which included:

  • Global Standardization: Identifying processes that could be standardized globally to ensure consistency across the organization.

  • Regional Customization: Determining processes that needed to be tailored to specific regional requirements, such as compliance with local regulations and cultural differences.

  • Process Mapping: We mapped out the target processes, ensuring they aligned with the client’s objectives of efficiency and improved employee experience.


Phase 2: Development and Localization

The second phase focused on developing and implementing the new HR processes, ensuring that they adhered to the target architecture defined in Phase 1. Key activities included:

  • Regional-Specific Development: We worked with regional teams to develop HR processes that adhered to the target architecture but allowed for local variations where necessary.

  • Multi-Lingual Support: We implemented multi-lingual support to ensure that HR processes were accessible and user-friendly for the client’s diverse global workforce.

  • Automation: We integrated the newly defined processes into the existing HR systems, incorporating automation to reduce manual tasks, minimize errors, and improve efficiency.

  • Self-Service Options: We developed and enhanced self-service HR portals, enabling employees to access HR services easily, request time off, view benefits, and more.

Results


The implementation of this two-phase approach led to several key benefits for the client:

  • Process Efficiency: By standardizing HR processes globally and automating tasks, the client saw significant improvements in efficiency across business units.

  • Improved Employee Experience: The integration of user-friendly, multi-lingual self-service options allowed employees to manage their HR needs more easily, resulting in higher satisfaction and engagement.

  • Cost Reduction: The automation of manual HR tasks led to a reduction in operational costs and resource allocation, freeing up HR teams to focus on higher-value activities.

  • Regulatory Compliance: The client successfully accommodated regional regulations without compromising on global consistency, ensuring compliance in every region.

Conclusion


Through a detailed analysis of existing processes, the development of a standardized global HR framework, and regional customization to meet local needs, Dotsito helped the client optimize HR processes, reduce inefficiencies, and enhance employee experience. The client’s HR systems are now more integrated, automated, and user-friendly, leading to improved operational efficiency and a more satisfied and engaged global workforce.
